Active ingredients are skincare components that directly improve skin concern

Niacinamide

  • Niacinamide also called Vitamin B3 or nicotinamide is a water-soluble vitamin.

  • Niacinamide strengthens the skin barrier and boosts ceramide production, keeping moisture in and irritants out.
  • It reduces redness and inflammation and calms sensitive or acne-prone skin.
  • Niacinamide regulates oil production and helps balance oily skin.
  • Minimizes pores and smooths texture by improving skins elasticity.
  • Brightens skin and reduces appearance of dark spots and uneven tone.
Vitamin C

  • Vitamin C also called ascorbic acid is a water-soluble antioxidant used in skincare to protect and brighten the skin.

  • Brightens skin tone and reduces dark spots, hyperpigmentation, and dullness.
  • Stimulates collagen production, improves firmness and reduces fine lines.
  • Protects against environmental damage and neutralizes free radicals from UV exposure and pollution.
  • Supports skin healing and helps repair damage and even out texture.
Ceramides

  • Ceramides are lipids, also known as fats naturally found in your skin that make up a key part of the skin barrier. They help keep your skin hydrated and protected.

  • Strengthen the skin barrier and locks in moisture and keeps irritants out.
  • Prevent dryness and flakiness. Its essential is you want hydrated and smooth skin.
  • Supports skin repair and helps heal and maintain healthy skin.
  • Reduces sensitivity and makes a calmer, less reactive skin.
Retinol

  • Retinol is a derivative of Vitamin A and a powerful ingredient used in skincare for anti-aging and skin renewal.

  • Retinol boosts cell turnover and speeds up shedding of old skin cells and promotes new ones.
  • It stimulates collagen production and helps reduce fine lines and improve firmness.
  • Retinol smooths texture, unclogs pores and can reduce acne and rough patches.
  • It fades hyperpigmentation, evens out skin tone and reduces dark spots.
Sunscreen

  • Sunscreen is a product that protects your skin from harmful UV rays. In protecting the skin we talk about both UVA and UVB rays.

  • UVA is “The Aging Rays.”

  • They have longer wavelength and penetrates deep into the skin.
  • They cause premature aging such as wrinkles, sagging, dark spots.
  • UVA rays are present all year and can penetrate glass. Thats why I recommend using sunscreen everyday even if you are just inside the whole day.

  • UVB are “The Burning Rays.”

  • They have shorter wavelength and affects the outer layer of the skin.
  • They cause sunburn.
  • UVB plays a major factor in skin cancer.
  • They are stronger in summer and at higher altitudes but also present all year.
Peptides

  • Peptides are short chains of amino acids which is the building blocks of proteins. They signal your skin to repair, renew, and produce more collagen and elastin.

  • Peptides boost collagen & elastin and helps firm and plump the skin
  • It smooth fine lines and wrinkles and improves skins youthful look.
  • Strengthen skin barrier and supports resilience and hydration.
  • Peptides aid in repair and promote healing and overall skin health.
